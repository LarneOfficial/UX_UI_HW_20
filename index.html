Overview

What is it?

Echo is transparency, simplicity, and personalization to find the music you want in the places you love.

The Problem

Concertgoers often have trouble and frustrations when trying to buy tickets, research venues, or discover new events. Many find themselves frustrated at hidden fees, confused about venue policies, or lost when looking for information on smaller, local events.

The Solution

We believe that by creating a more transparent, accessible, and easy to use app for concertgoers, we will increase awareness and attendance while helping users achieve a more enjoyable and streamlined concert planning experience.

Research

Our user is Kyle Headbanger, a 24 year old single male who works as a software developer. His job offers him paid time off, and as an avid music enjoyer, he seeks to fill his days off with live music experiences, either solo or with friends.

Our user research was conducted and recorded via 5 one-on-one interviews, and assisted by demographic information collected from a survey created with Google Forms. Additionally we also conducted and recorded 5 user tests with our prototype to help us understand the shortcomings and strong points of our prototype. Some of the key findings we derived from the interviews and survey include: 1. Users typically hear about upcoming concerts via social media, and through their favorite artist’s brand websites 2. Users are very frequently prevented from going to concerts that they would’ve loved to attend -  3. Primarily because of high travel costs, and secondly because they didn’t even know the concert was happening!

    The surprise me button is a key feature of the Echo app that needed a little more highlighting. We added descriptive captions to guide users into using the button and understanding its purpose. Basic sizing and inconsistency issues confused the testers, which resulted in re-editing the prototype. Making the search bar and feature more obvious and accessible is also a priority. Making it locked in as you scroll on top of the main page, as well as a main navigation bar feature helps the user to access it easily.

Final Prototype

Conclusion

Echo Concerts & Events brings concert awareness and aids in the planning process by providing to users an easy to use app with information on local bands and venues. Based on the research, we had to prioritize users' needs and dismiss prior preconceived notions relating to concert-goers' experiences. For example, most participants indicated they do not mind sitting or standing at concerts. Creating a filter or feature based on that would have been less relevant to the actual needs, which were not able to make a concert due to budget concerns and lack of awareness.
